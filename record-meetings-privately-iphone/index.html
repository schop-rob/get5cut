The compliance problem with cloud meeting recorders

Every time you use a cloud transcription service, you're creating a data processing relationship. That means:

  • A third party now holds recordings of confidential business discussions
  • You need a Data Processing Agreement (DPA) under GDPR
  • SOC 2 auditors will ask about it
  • Your legal team may not have approved it
  • If the service is breached, your meeting content is exposed
  • Client NDAs may prohibit sharing recordings with third parties — even for "processing"

On-device processing eliminates all of these concerns. The data never leaves your phone, so there's no third-party involvement to audit.

Who needs private meeting recording

Legal professionals

Client meetings, case strategy discussions, settlement negotiations. Attorney-client privilege doesn't mix well with third-party cloud processing.

Healthcare

Clinical team meetings, patient case reviews, administrative discussions involving PHI. HIPAA compliance is simpler when recordings stay on-device.

Finance and banking

Investment discussions, client advisory meetings, compliance reviews. Material non-public information shouldn't exist on a transcription startup's servers.

HR and people operations

Performance reviews, disciplinary meetings, compensation discussions. Employee data requires careful handling under GDPR and local employment law.

Startups and M&A

Board meetings, fundraising calls, acquisition discussions. Anything that's pre-announcement or under NDA.

The 5cut workflow for meetings

  1. Open the recorder — tap record when the meeting starts. 5cut captures audio with a live waveform so you can see it's working
  2. Live transcription — see words appear in real-time as people speak, entirely on-device
  3. Speaker identification — up to 4 speakers are automatically identified and color-coded. Rename them after the meeting
  4. Trim silence — remove the gaps between agenda items, the "can you hear me" moments, the waiting-for-latecomers silence
  5. Export — transcript as text, subtitled video, or SRT. Everything stays in your Files app or goes where you send it — nowhere else

Frequently asked questions

Does the transcription really work offline?

Yes. After downloading a language model once (40-700 MB depending on the engine), transcription works with no internet. You can verify by enabling airplane mode.

How accurate is on-device transcription?

5cut offers four transcription engines. WhisperKit (based on OpenAI's Whisper) achieves near-cloud accuracy for most languages. For English business meetings with clear audio, expect 90-95% accuracy.

Can I record phone calls?

5cut records from the microphone — it captures in-person meetings or speakerphone calls. It cannot tap into the phone's audio system to record the other party directly (iOS prevents this for privacy). For video calls, use the speakerphone and place your phone nearby.

How long can I record?

Limited only by your iPhone's available storage. A 1-hour meeting at standard quality is approximately 30 MB.
