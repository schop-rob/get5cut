How to Remove Silence from Lecture Recordings

If you record lectures — or download them from your university's LMS — you know the problem: a 90-minute recording has about 30 minutes of silence. Professors pause between slides, students ask quiet questions off-mic, and there are long gaps during live demonstrations.

That silence wastes your study time. Watching at 2× speed helps, but it only compresses silence — it doesn't remove it. 5cut removes it.

90 → 55 min
Average time saved on a typical university lecture

Why silence removal beats 2× speed

Speeding up a video makes everything faster — the silence and the speech. If a professor already speaks quickly, 2× makes them impossible to follow, especially in a foreign language.

Silence removal keeps speech at normal speed and only cuts the parts where nothing is being said. You hear every word clearly, but the dead air between sentences and slides is gone.

How 5cut works

  1. Import your lecture from Photos, Files, or share it directly from another app
  2. 5cut analyzes the audio and color-codes the waveform — green for speech, red for silence
  3. Adjust the threshold with a slider, or pick a Smart Mode preset (Gentle, Moderate, or Aggressive)
  4. Export — the processed video is saved to your camera roll, ready to watch

Built for students

Finals week

You have 20 hours of lecture recordings to review. At 2× speed, that's still 10 hours. With 5cut removing the silence, it's 6 — because the pauses are gone, not just faster.

Foreign language lectures

If you're studying abroad or taking courses in a second language, you need to hear every word clearly. 5cut removes silence without touching the speech, so you can concentrate on understanding the content rather than enduring dead air.

Online courses and Zoom recordings

Zoom lectures and MOOC recordings often have especially long pauses — the professor answering chat questions, screen sharing lag, or simply waiting for students to catch up. 5cut handles all of these.

Also generates subtitles

5cut can transcribe your lecture in over 30 languages directly on your device. Burn subtitles into the video for easy review, or export as an SRT file. This is especially useful for lectures in a foreign language where you want to read along while listening.

Privacy first
