Offline Meeting Notes on iPhone

You need meeting notes, but your company's security policy won't let you use Otter, Fireflies, or any cloud transcription service. Or you're in a conference room with no WiFi. Or you're on a flight. Or you simply don't trust a third party with your meeting content.

5cut generates meeting transcripts entirely offline on your iPhone. Record, transcribe, identify speakers — no internet connection required at any point.

How offline meeting notes work

  1. Download a language model once — this is the only step that needs internet (40-700 MB, one-time download)
  2. Record the meeting — tap record. Works in airplane mode, underground, anywhere
  3. Transcribe on-device — the AI runs on your iPhone's Neural Engine. A 1-hour meeting transcribes in 3-8 minutes depending on the model you choose
  4. Speaker separation — up to 4 speakers are automatically identified and labeled. Rename them to "Sarah (PM)", "David (Eng)", etc.
  5. Export — copy the transcript, share as text, or export with timestamps

Why go offline for meeting notes

Corporate security policies

Many companies prohibit uploading internal discussions to third-party services. IT security reviews for new SaaS tools take months. 5cut needs no security review — data never leaves the device, so there's no third-party risk to evaluate.

Regulated industries

Finance, healthcare, legal, defense — these sectors have strict data handling requirements. On-device processing means no DPA, no vendor risk assessment, no compliance audit for the transcription tool.

Client confidentiality

Consulting firms, agencies, and law firms can't send client meeting recordings to external services without violating NDAs. 5cut keeps everything local.

Unreliable connectivity

Conference rooms in basements, meetings during travel, offsite retreats with spotty WiFi. Offline transcription just works regardless of your connection.

What you get

  • Full transcript with timestamps — "00:03:42 Speaker 1: We need to revisit the Q3 budget..."
  • Speaker labels — color-coded, renameable, up to 4 distinct speakers
  • Silence removal — strip the "ums", long pauses, and dead air from the recording. A 60-minute meeting becomes 45 minutes of actual discussion
  • Multiple export formats — plain text, SRT subtitles, or video with burned-in captions
  • 30+ languages — multilingual teams can transcribe in the meeting's language

Practical scenarios

Weekly standup

Record the 15-minute standup, get a transcript with who said what, share with the team member who was out sick. No cloud service needed.

Client discovery call

Record on speakerphone. After the call, transcribe and search for the specific requirements the client mentioned. The recording stays on your phone, not on a transcription company's server.

Board meeting

Sensitive strategic discussions that absolutely cannot be uploaded to third-party infrastructure. Record, transcribe locally, share the transcript only with attendees.

International team meeting

Your team speaks German in meetings but the US office needs notes in English. Record the meeting, transcribe in German, then you have a searchable text to work from.

Compared to alternatives

  • Otter.ai / Fireflies / Fathom — all require internet and upload to cloud servers
  • Apple Voice Memos + transcription (iOS 26) — limited to Apple's built-in engine, no speaker labels, no silence removal
  • Whisper on a laptop — requires technical setup, can't record from phone in meetings easily
  • 5cut — one app that records, transcribes (4 engines to choose from), identifies speakers, and trims silence. All offline
