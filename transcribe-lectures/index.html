Transcribe Lecture Recordings on iPhone

Need subtitles for a lecture in another language? 5cut transcribes audio directly on your iPhone — no cloud, no upload, no account. Just accurate captions in over 30 languages.

Why on-device transcription matters

Most transcription services require you to upload your recordings to a server. That means waiting for processing, worrying about privacy, and often paying per minute of audio. 5cut does everything locally on your device using Apple's speech recognition, so:

  • Your recordings never leave your phone — no privacy concerns with sensitive university content
  • No internet required — download the language model once, then transcribe anywhere
  • No per-minute charges — transcribe as many lectures as you want

Two ways to use subtitles

Burn into video

Subtitles are rendered directly onto the video frames. The exported file plays with visible captions on any device or player — no separate subtitle file needed. Great for sharing or reviewing on the go.

Export as SRT

Export a standard SRT subtitle file that you can use with any video player, upload to YouTube, or import into editing software. Each subtitle is timestamped to the original video.

Combine with silence removal

5cut is primarily a silence removal tool. After trimming the dead air from your lecture, you can also generate subtitles for the processed video. The workflow is:

  1. Import your lecture recording
  2. Remove silence (automatic or fine-tuned)
  3. Generate subtitles in the lecture's language
  4. Export with burned-in subtitles or as SRT + video

Speaker identification

If your lecture has multiple speakers — a professor and a teaching assistant, or a seminar with student presentations — 5cut can identify up to 4 different speakers. Each speaker is color-coded in the transcript, and you can rename them for easy reference.
